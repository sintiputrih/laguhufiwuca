Nick Robinson’s acting career

At 11, Nick took part in a theatrical adaptation of the novel A Christmas Carol written by Charles Dickens. After his impressive performances, a family friend who was also present suggested to his parents that Nick meet legendary Hollywood talent scout Matt Casella. If there was anyone to give a verdict on Nick’s abilities, it was Matt. He has an impressive track record of talent scouting and discernment to determine which new qualified kid has what it takes to make it big in Hollywood.

Matt’s verdict was simple. Nick was a star. Not only his skills, he was impressed with his mannerisms and charismatic demeanor. He recommended him to a few agencies and Nick ended up signing with The Savage Agency in Los Angeles. His signing with the agency coincided with the Writers Guild of America strike of 2007-2008, and as a result he had to return to Washington with his parents where he continued to play gigs around DC. In 2009, he auditioned for a television show and was given the role of Ryder Scanlon in the sitcom ABC Family. Melissa & Joey. He played the nephew of Melissa Joan Hart’s character.
