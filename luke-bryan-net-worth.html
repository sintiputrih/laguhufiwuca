Who Is Luke Bryan?

Luke Bryan is one of the most popular and successful country music artists of his generation.

He worked as a songwriter for other artists, such as Travis Tritt and Billy Currington.

Luke Bryan then signed a deal with Capitol Nashville and released his debut album I’ll Stay Me in 2007.

The album featured the singles All My Friends Say and Country Man.

He followed it up with his second album Doin’ My Thing in 2009, which included the hits Do I and Rain Is a Good Thing.

Luke Bryan Career

Luke Bryan achieved his breakthrough with his third album Tailgates & Tanlines in 2011, which reached No. 1 on the Billboard Top Country Albums chart and No. 2 on the Billboard 200 chart.

The album spawned four No. 1 singles: I Don’t Want This Night to End, Drunk on You, Kiss Tomorrow Goodbye, and The Only Way I Know (with Jason Aldean and Eric Church).

He also released four EPs under the title Spring Break, which featured songs about partying and having fun.

Luke Bryan continued his success with his fourth album Crash My Party in 2013, which sold over two million copies and won the Academy of Country Music Award for Album of the Year.

The album produced six No. 1 singles: Crash My Party, That’s My Kind of Night, Drink a Beer, Play It Again, Roller Coaster, and I See You.

He also released his first compilation album Spring Break…Here to Party, which topped the Billboard 200 chart.

Luke Bryan’s fifth album Kill the Lights in 2015 became his third consecutive No. 1 album on the Billboard 200 chart and his best-selling album to date.

It also broke the record for the most consecutive No. 1 singles on the Billboard Country Airplay chart with six: Kick the Dust Up, Strip It Down, Home Alone Tonight (featuring Karen Fairchild), Huntin’, Fishin’ and Lovin’ Every Day, Move, and Fast.
