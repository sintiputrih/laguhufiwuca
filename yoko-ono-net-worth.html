Japanese artist, songwriter, peace activist, and filmmaker Yoko Ono’s net worth is a staggering $700 million.

After her husband’s murder, Yoko became active in several charity endeavors.

Yoko Ono | Houses, Cars, & Artwork

Houses

In 1980, Yoko and her husband bought a 14,000 square feet mansion in Palm Beach’s South Ocean Boulevard. She paid $725,000 for the property and sold it six years later after renovation.

She currently resides on the seventh level of the Dakota apartment, the same large nine-room apartment she used to live in with John.

She also rents a flat in the West 72nd Street building for guests.

In addition, Yoko Ono still owns a 600 acres vacation home near Franklin, New York but hasn’t been there for a while.

Furthermore, she sold her 5,700-square-foot Greenwich Village penthouse for $8.3 million in 2014.

Artwork

Before she entered the world of music and glamour, she was a concept artist, creating a buzz with her artworks.

In the early 1980s, Ono began her participatory art installation Wish Tree, where visitors were invited to write their wishes on small pieces of paper and connect them to the tree.

The artwork is continuing and has acquired respect and recognition in recent years.

Similarly, another famous conceptual artwork of hers is Cut Piece. In 1964, she sat motionless on stage and let people cut her clothes. The provocative act was an early piece of feminist art.

In addition, Smile Film is her ambitious art project film. She wants to create a movie with happy face snaps of every human individual on the globe.

Her #smilesfilm project is a vision of mass participation that invites people to contribute smiling photos to Instagram and Twitter. The project is still going on.

Furthermore, she has made other famous arts like Grapefruit Book, Yes Painting, Half a Room, My Mommy is Beautiful, Pieces of Sky, and Smoke Painting.

Yoko Ono | Lifestyle, and Vacation

Lifestyle

Yoko Ono’s net worth is a massive $700 million, and hence she lives an affluent life.

She was born to traditional nobles in Japan. Even though she was born in a rich family, she had a difficult upbringing.

Yoko Ono has often attributed her musicality and creativity to her lonely childhood due to the negligence from her mother and separation from her father.

She is a renowned peace activist. Ono became a pacifist after seeing the continuous devastation of World War II. She and her family had to beg for food during the war, so she understands what it’s like to be a war victim.

Yoko Ono has married three times in her life, the first one in 1955 to Toschi Ichiyanagi, a Juilliard student who shared her interest in experimental music.

In 1961, she divorced him and married Tony Cox, an American jazz pianist. They had a daughter together, but she lost custody of her daughter after the divorce.

Yoko met with her daughter when she was 22 years old, but they are still estranged.

Her last marriage was with John Lennon in 1969. Yoko was madly in love with him and lived with him until he died.

Nowadays, Yoko Ono is rarely seen in public and spends the majority of her time in a wheelchair, requiring round-the-clock care.

Yoko Ono was admitted to the hospital in 2016 after suffering from what was initially thought to be a stroke, although she later revealed it was a severe flu.

In 2020, she handed over all her business affairs, including eight companies linked to John and Beatles to her son, Sean.

Vacation

Yoko Ono has traveled all around the world in her life. She has taken a vacation in the most exotic locations on the planet.

When she was with John, they traveled to Scotland in 1969. Similarly, they traveled to Paris, Gibraltar, Amsterdam, Vienna, The Bahamas, and Montreal.

But in recent years, she hasn’t traveled much due to her age and difficulty walking.

Yoko Charity | Charity & Activism 

Charity

In 2018, Yoko Ono donated $35,000 to a charity formed by her late husband, the Spirit Foundations. 

Similarly, she donated $250,000 to Montefiore Medical Center in The Bronx to support frontline healthcare workers during the outset of the Coronavirus outbreak in New York.

In addition, she has also made considerable donations to the arts, peace, and disaster relief.

Yoko recently donated $50,000 to the West Side Campaign Against Hunger, which helped thousands of out-of-work and hungry citizens in her Upper West Side neighborhood during the pandemic.

Moreover, she also supports charities like Aid Still Required, Free Arts NYC, Soles4Soul, The Doe Fund, and The Gorilla Organization.

Activism

Yoko Ono’s political activism revolves around the peace and equality campaign she and John Lennon initiated in 1969.

Yoko’s most interesting protest is her honeymoon with John, called The bed-ins.

They invited the press to their Hilton hotel room in Amsterdam for their honeymoon, where they laid in bed for a week as a kind of nonviolent protest against the Vietnam War.

The duo elevated the concept of a sit-in to a new level of experimentation, transforming it into a peaceful performance.

Similarly, in 2012, she joined with the group Occupy With Art to further the cause of world peace in support of the Occupy Wall Street Movement.

In addition, to emphasize her support for marriage equality and homosexual rights, Yoko modified the lyrics and title of her song “Every Man Has a Woman Who Loves Him” to “Everyman/Everywoman.”

Furthermore, Yoko Ono funded a New York anti-fracking billboard in favor of environmental activism.

Yoko Ono | Career

In 1960, Yoko Ono rented an inexpensive loft in downtown Manhattan and used it as a studio. She held a series of shows to showcase her avant-garde arts.

After producing several praiseworthy artworks, she eventually started getting recognized in the art community.

She met John Lennon at an exhibition of her work in a London gallery in 1966. The two began working together on experimental films and recordings in 1968.

They released the experiment album “Unfinished Music No. 1: Two Virgins” (1968), which included a controversial photograph of them naked.

Yoko Ono also contributed to songs by The Beatles. She sang backup vocals on “Birthday” and had a lead vocal line on “The Continuing Story of Bungalow Bill.”

In 1981, Yoko released “Season of Glass,” a critically acclaimed album. Similarly, in 1984, Ono and Lennon’s final album, “Milk and Honey” was released.

Her most successful solo album, “Starpeace” (1985), was released as a concept album. The next year, she went on a world tour.
