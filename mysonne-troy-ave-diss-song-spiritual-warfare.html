Mysonne Declares 'Spiritual Warfare' On Troy Ave With New JAY-Z-Sampling Diss Song

Publish date: 2024-06-07

Mysonne has taken aim at Troy Ave yet again on a new diss song while proclaiming their rift is more than just beef — it’s “spiritual warfare.”

The Bronx, New York rapper shared a minute-long snippet of the diss track on Instagram on Tuesday (April 25), which finds him airing out Troy for being a “snitch” and biting 50 Cent’s style over a trap-infused flip of JAY-Z and Eminem’s “Renegade.”

“This is for real n-ggas only, we ain’t condoning no sucka shit, nah we ain’t fuckin’ with it/ Weak-ass n-ggas that play tough then run and snitch/ Don’t come around with us with that sucka shit,” Mysonne raps on the chorus to open the song.

He then spits: “Now they praising the rats, the integrity gone/ When a man giving you facts, you’ll never be wrong/ When the clown performing, you just let him amuse/ When a G’s living with honor, he never gon’ lose/ Nothing to prove, n-gga, I done already proved that/ Now you in trouble ’cause My’s getting his groove back.”

“You ain’t just take the 50 dude’s raps, you took the n-gga whole style, wow! How you do that?/ Damn, it’s no more shame, shit, the game is over, it’s no more games/ Same n-gga that moved bricks snitched to your honor/ To get his man killed and talked slick ’bout his momma.”

At the end of his scathing verse, The General raises the stakes by describing his feud with Troy Ave as a “war” between good and evil: “Tryna play nice but I see we at war/ It’s righteous versus evil and they uppin’ the score.”

“My bad I just realized that wasn’t the Final Mixed Version.. #IrapMyAssOff,” he added in his Instagram caption. “FYI A Rat can’t beat me a NOTHING.. THIS IS FOR REAL N!&&@$ ONLY.. This ain’t Beef it’s Spiritual Warfare.. #GodsWork #BoycottBlackMurder.”

The diss track is just the latest salvo in Mysonne and Troy Ave’s long-running feud, which has been reignited in the wake of Taxstone being found guilty of shooting and killing Troy’s bodyguard, Ronald “Banga” McPhatter, in 2016.

After taking the stand and testifying against Tax during the trial earlier this month, Troy seemingly pre-empted the backlash from his rivals by reworking his 2021 song “Money Dance 1-2-3” as a diss track aimed at Mysonne, along with Taxstone, Joe Budden, Maino, DJ Vlad and others.

The General fired back with a diss song of his own called “Rolland (Troy Ave) It Ain’t All About The Money,” on which he gave the Bricks In My Backpack rapper a stern dressing-down.

“For you, it’s all about the money my n-gga/ Keep making jokes — I guess this shit is funny my n-gga/ But you got your man killed, got shot how many times?/ You don’t see how your karma keep coming, my n-gga?” he rapped while jacking Troy’s “All About The Money” beat.

The pair’s issues date back to 2017 when Troy Ave appeared on The Breakfast Club and compared himself to 2Pac over the 2016 shooting that killed his bodyguard. Mysonne later took to Instagram to proudly label Troy a “bozo” for even suggesting he was close to ‘Pac.
