Kevin Byrne (mayor) obituary

Publish date: 2024-06-07

The passing of Kevin Michael Byrne on September 21, 2023, marks the end of an era for Cairns, Australia, and the broader political landscape. As a two-term Mayor of Cairns, military veteran, and distinguished citizen, Kevin Byrne left an indelible mark on the hearts of those who knew him and the community he served.

A Life of Service and Dedication

Born in 1949 in Lae, Territory of New Guinea, Kevin Byrne’s life was a testament to service and dedication. He attended St Joseph’s College, Nudgee, and embarked on a military career that would become a cornerstone of his life. Byrne’s unwavering allegiance to Australia led him to the Australian Army, where he served from 1969 to 1987.

During his military service, Kevin Byrne’s leadership qualities shone brightly, culminating in his appointment as the Senior Instructor at the Royal Military College, Duntroon. Notably, he received the Sword of Honour for Leadership during his time at OCS Portsea in 1969, a recognition of his exceptional qualities.

Political Legacy in Cairns

While Kevin Byrne’s military career was exemplary, his contributions extended far beyond the military realm. He entered the world of politics, serving as the Mayor of Cairns in two non-consecutive terms. From 1992 to 1995 and then again from 2000 to 2008, Byrne’s leadership had a profound impact on the city he loved.

During his tenure as Mayor, Kevin Byrne championed numerous initiatives aimed at improving the lives of Cairns residents. His dedication to the region’s development and prosperity earned him the respect and admiration of his constituents.

Honors and Recognition

In addition to his military and political achievements, Kevin Byrne was the recipient of significant civilian awards. He was honored with the Papua New Guinea Independence Medal in 2000, acknowledging his contributions to the region’s growth and stability. The Australian Centenary Medal, presented in 2001, further underscored his dedication to the Australian community.
