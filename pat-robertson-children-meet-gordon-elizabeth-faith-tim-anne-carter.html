Early Life And Career of Pat Robertson

Marion Gordon “Pat” Robertson was a media tycoon, Southern Baptist clergyman, political pundit, religious broadcaster, and presidential contender in the United States.

He died on June 8, 2023. Robertson was a prominent Republican Party figure who promoted a conservative Christian worldview.

Within Protestant evangelicalism, he was connected to the Charismatic movement. Both Regent University and the Christian Broadcasting Network (CBN) were led by him.

In addition to founding CBN, Regent University, Operation Blessing International Relief and Development Corporation, and International Family Entertainment Inc.

(ABC Family Channel/Freeform), the American Center for Law & Justice (ACLJ), the Founders Inn and Conference Center, and the Christian Coalition, Robertson’s career spanned more than five decades.

Robertson was also a best-selling author and the former host of The 700 Club, a Christian news program that was once broadcast live on weekdays on Freeform (previously ABC Family), on channels across the country, and on CBN network affiliates around the world from CBN studios.

On the 60th anniversary of the first broadcast of what would later become CBN on October 1, 1961, Robertson announced his retirement from the 700 Club at the age of 91.

Robertson, a Southern Baptist who served as an ordained preacher for many years and is the son of U.S. Senator A. Willis Robertson, adheres to a charismatic theology that is not often held by Southern Baptists.

He ran an unsuccessful effort to become the 1988 Republican presidential nominee.

He was no longer employed by any church in an official capacity as a result of his campaign for public office.

Robertson is still a contentious figure.

Pat Robertson children have a good legacy on which they can build, left by their father.

While he rose to prominence as a prominent public advocate for conservative Christianity in the United States and abroad, his resistance to a number of progressive causes, such as feminism, LGBT rights, and the right to abortion, has drawn much criticism.
