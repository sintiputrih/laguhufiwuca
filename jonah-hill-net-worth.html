Career

The actor/comedian was born on December 20, 1983, in Los Angeles to Richard Feldstein and Sharon Lyn. His father was a tour accountant for the famous band Guns N Roses while his mother was a costume stylist.

While growing up, Jonah loved watching Simpsons and even used to call himself “a Simpson’s kid.”

Jonah Hill’s family was affluent, and they lived in a rich area of Los Angeles. Hill attended Bard College after high school and later shifted to New York to study drama.

In college, Jonah became friends with the son of Dustin Hoffman, Jake, who later introduced Jonah to his father. Back in college, Hill used to write and perform his plays.

Later Hoffman offered him an audition for a role in I Heart Huckabees, a 2004 film from which Jonah Hill debuted in acting career.

Then in 2007, Hill received his first lead role in Superbad, which became very popular, and following it, his career rose. Moreover, in 2008, he was ranked at the 21st position on Entertainment Weekly’s “30 under 30” actors.

He has also shared the screen with Brad Pitt in the movie Moneyball and Leonardo DiCaprio in The Wolf of Wall Street.
