Who is Jack Eichel?

Jack Eichel is an American professional ice hockey player. He currently plays as a centreman for the Buffalo Sabres of the National Hockey League (NHL). He won a gold medal representing the United States at the 2014 IIHF World Under-18 Championship. He was the team’s youngest player to play in the tournament.

Jack Eichel: Age, Parents, Ethnicity

Jack was born on October 28, 1996, in North Chelmsford, Massachusetts, USA. He is 26 years old. His nationality is American and he is of North American ethnicity.

He is the son of American parents Anne Eichel(mother) and Bob Eichel(father).

In which university did Jack Eichel graduate?

He was educated at Boston University, where he played for the college team.

Jack Eichel: Professional Life and Career

Jack played college hockey for one season at Boston University and won the Hobey Baker Award in 2015, becoming the second freshman ever to earn the honor. He played for the USA Hockey National Team Development Program team during the 2012–13 and 2013–14 seasons.

During the 2013-14 season, he was named to the United States Hockey League Second All-Star Team. He was selected second overall in the 2015 NHL Entry Draft by the Buffalo Sabres of NHL on June 26, 2015. On July 1, 2015, he signed a three-year entry-level contract with the Sabres. He scored his first NHL goal in his first game in the NHL against the Ottawa Senators, becoming the youngest player to score in the history of the Sabres.

At the age of 15, Eichel represented the United States at the 2012 Winter Youth Olympics. He won a bronze medal with Team USA at the 2013 World U-17 Hockey Challenge and a silver medal at the 2013 IIHF World U18 Championships. The following season, he helped Team USA win the gold medal at the 2014 IIHF World U18 Championships.

He competed as a 17-year-old as Team USA’s youngest player at the 2014 World Junior Ice Hockey Championships. Eichel has recently been selected to the 2017 Team USA IIHF World Championship roster.
